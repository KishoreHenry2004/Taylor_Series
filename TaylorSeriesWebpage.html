Taylor Series

Exploring what is Taylor Series using graphical aid!

Taylor Series is a tool for approximating non-polynomial equations such as sin x, e^x, etc to polynomial functions.

Their approximation becomes more accurate as the number of terms increases.

The approximations are done because polynomial functions are easier to handle!


The general expression of a polynomial function:

P(x) = k0+k1x+k2x2+k3x3+k4x4+...

Principle behind the approximation of non-polynomial functions using Taylor Series

Mathematically approximating a function f(x) with another polynomial P(x) implies that the value of f(x) and P(x) and their successive differentiation at an arbitrary point x0 overlaps!

Here x0 is the point where the taylor series of the function is to be centered at. For simplicity, consider x0 = 0.

f(x)|x=0 = P(x)|x=0

f1(x)|x=0 = P1(x)|x=0

f2(x)|x=0 = P2(x)|x=0

f3(x)|x=0 = P3(x)|x=0

f4(x)|x=0 = P4(x)|x=0

Sustituting the values for constants (k's) in the general expression of a polynomial function:
